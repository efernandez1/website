Hello World! If you've stumbled upon my website I must first take a moment to thank you for entering a part of my world. I am a big data analyst at a Fortune 50 company in the great state of Colorado. This last sentence might be a lie unless I get a job offer! This is my first website and I will be using it as a place to learn computer and data science and other interesting topics.

What exactly is a data scientist? I've heard it described as someone who knows more computer science than a statistician and someone who knows more statistics than a computer scientist. I think this is accurate because it highlights that data science is really a combination of statistics, computer science, and business expertise. It allows businesses to leverage their data to drive efficiencies and save money or make more money by delivering a better produce. For this reason, I am going to create a section that is devoted to data analysis on kaggle competitions or important and interesting topics.

The next section of my website will be similar a blog where I write about my journey to becoming a data analyst and hopefully one day, a data scientist. Prior to my last job I did a few code academy courses on HTML, CSS, PHP, and Ruby. I did not complete a single class and usually completed less than 50%. I didn't have a computer science background so things really weren't making sense until my last job where I started as an Excel monkey and then automated parts of my job with VBA. Out of necessity I turned to R and have become more interested in learning general coding practices and understanding algorithms like bubble sort, binary searches, or whatever. So I wanted to create a section where I could blog about how I've learned to code and the difficulties I've faced with things and the light bulb moments when things began to click.

This last section will be something I reference often. It will be a compilation of useful resources such as wiki links, Youtube videos, or other educational sites that I've found worthwhile.
